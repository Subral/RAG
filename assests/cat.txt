A cat is a **small, typically furry, carnivorous mammal** known for its unique anatomy and behavior. Cats are members of the family **Felidae** and are often referred to as **house cats** when kept as pets, distinguishing them from the wild members of the family.

## 🐾 Physical Characteristics

* **Senses:** Cats possess exceptional senses. Their **hearing** is highly acute, capable of detecting a much wider range of frequencies than humans or dogs. Their **vision** is best in low light, aided by a reflective layer called the *tapetum lucidum*.
* **Anatomy:** Key features include a **highly flexible body**, quick reflexes, retractable claws, and teeth adapted for killing small prey.
* **Whiskers (Vibrissae):** These are highly sensitive touch receptors that help cats navigate and assess the size of small openings.
* **Size:** Most house cats weigh between 4 and 5 kg (8.8 and 11 lb), though this varies widely by breed and individual health.

---

## 习 Behavior and Habits

* **Grooming:** Cats spend a significant portion of their time self-grooming, which helps keep their coat clean, regulates body temperature, and promotes circulation.
* **Vocalization:** They communicate through a variety of sounds, including **meowing**, purring, hissing, growling, and trilling.
* **Sleep:** Cats are known to sleep for long periods, often **12 to 16 hours per day**, a trait linked to their predatory nature. They conserve energy between hunting expeditions.
* **Play and Hunting:** Even domesticated cats retain strong hunting instincts, often expressed through play with toys. Their hunting is typically a solitary activity.

---

## 🧬 Domestication and Lifespan

* **History:** The domestication of the cat is thought to have begun in the **Near East** around 7,500 BC, likely originating from the African wildcat (*Felis silvestris lybica*).
* **Lifespan:** The average lifespan of a well-cared-for indoor house cat is **12 to 15 years**, though some can live into their 20s. Outdoor cats typically have shorter lifespans due to environmental hazards.
